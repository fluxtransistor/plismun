Prague

Dubbed the heart of Europe for both its geographical location and rich cultural heritage, Prague is one of the most beautiful cities on the continent. The architecture lets you take a trip through time as you witness the centuries of growth that this metropolis has experienced. Common examples include the Prague Castle, Astronomical Clock and the National Theatre, but the truth is that wherever you walk you are bound to experience the charm of being surrounded by buildings that are centuries old.

Prague offers a wide variety of activities including paddle-boating on the Vltava River, which is not a problem even in January. Alternatively, you can take a tour through the National Museum or just walk around the popular landmarks and cross the Charles Bridge.

Wandering the labyrinth of streets, you can stumble across small cafes, fascinating antique shops and galleries. Perhaps you might spot street art by an artist like David Černý. Prague is home to countless parks nestled in the city, like Letná, Kampa or Petřín Park. These green havens contrast the busy, bustling nature of the more frequented tourist areas.

Park Lane International School is located in Prague 1, the very centre of the city. Just down the hill from Prague Castle, across the street from the Senate and a minute's walk away from the beautiful Wallenstein Garden, our school has an unrivalled location. With a tram and metro station, accessibility is no problem as well.



Although the COVID-19 pandemic made the world go through some difficult times, Prague is not very heavily affected by this outbreak and the Czech Republic only had 0.45 active cases per 1000 people at the peak, in early April of 2020. The city will definitely be safe to travel to by January 2021 and face masks are likely to be unnecessary.
