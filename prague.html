Prague

Prague is the epicentre of Europe, renown for its deep, rich history and culture. Thriving for hundreds upon hundreds of years, this city has bloomed to become one of the continent's most beautiful cities. Architecture spanning centuries can be found all throughout Prague, whether it be at Prague Castle, the National Theatre, the Astronomical Clock or the wide variety of churches and cathedrals.

Prague has rapidly been developing and evolving over the years. This can be seen in the plethora of activities available. From a tour inside the grand and newly-renovated National Museum to paddleboating on the Vltava River under a gentle sun, this city can provide you with something to catch your interest.

Wandering the labyrinth of streets, you can stumble across small cafes, fascinating antique shops and galleries. Perhaps you might spot street art by an artist like David Černý. Prague is home to countless parks nestled in the city, like Letna, Kampa or Petřín Park. These green havens contrast the busy, bustling nature of the more frequented tourist areas.
